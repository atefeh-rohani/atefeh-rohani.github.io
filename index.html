Short Bio

  • Atefeh Rohani

    Applied Scientist | Formal Verification & AI Reliability

    • I am a Senior Researcher at the University of Bern specializing in the intersection of Formal Methods, Machine Learning, and Safety-Critical Systems. With over six years of experience and a PhD in Computer Science, my work focuses on enhancing the reliability and societal trust of autonomous systems, such as Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) and space software.

    My technical expertise spans the development of predictive AI models, particularly for time-series data, and the integration of formal verification with ML testing frameworks to ensure high-assurance software architectures. I have successfully spearheaded multidisciplinary R&D projects, transitioning complex models from simulation to real-world deployment, and have been awarded research funding from the Hasler foundation for my work on UAV reliability (collaboration with Sebastiano Panichella).

    Beyond research, I am an active educator and contributor to the scientific community, having served as a Teaching Assistant for courses in Advanced Algorithms, Computability and Complexity Theory and Justification Logic. Outside of the lab, I am a soprano in the Bahar Choir and enjoy hiking and biking through the Swiss Alps.
